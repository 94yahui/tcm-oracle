Individuals with a Yang Deficiency (Yang Xu) constitution typically experience a constant feeling of coldness, pale complexion, cold hands and feet, fatigue, and a preference for warm food and drinks.
For Yang Deficiency (Yang Xu), warming foods are highly recommended to supplement Kidney Yang and generate internal heat; beneficial foods include ginger, lamb, cinnamon, walnuts, chives, and shrimp.
People with a Yang Deficiency (Yang Xu) constitution must strictly avoid cold-natured and raw foods, such as watermelon, bitter melon, green tea, iced beverages, and raw salads, as these extinguish the body's digestive fire.
Individuals with a Yin Deficiency (Yin Xu) constitution often feel internal heat, experience night sweats, have a dry mouth and throat, dry skin, and prefer cool environments.
To nourish fluids and clear empty heat in a Yin Deficiency (Yin Xu) constitution, cooling and moisturizing foods are recommended, such as duck meat, lily bulb, silver ear mushroom, pears, tofu, and goji berries.
Those with a Yin Deficiency (Yin Xu) constitution should avoid spicy, drying, and hot-natured foods like chili, garlic, lamb, deep-fried meals, and coffee, which further deplete the body's internal moisture.
Individuals with a Phlegm-Dampness (Tan Shi) constitution often feel physical heaviness, have oily skin and hair, tend to carry excess weight, and experience sluggish digestion or sticky stools.
To clear dampness and strengthen the spleen for a Phlegm-Dampness (Tan Shi) constitution, recommended foods include Job's tears (coix seed), adzuki beans, winter melon, white radish, and kelp.
People with a Phlegm-Dampness (Tan Shi) constitution must avoid sweets, fatty meats, greasy foods, dairy products like cheese, and rich fruits like durian, as these contribute to phlegm accumulation.
Individuals with a Damp-Heat (Shi Re) constitution are prone to acne, oily skin, a bitter taste in the mouth, irritability, and may experience incomplete bowel movements.
For a Damp-Heat (Shi Re) constitution, it is essential to consume heat-clearing and damp-resolving foods, including mung beans, celery, cucumber, bitter melon, water chestnut, and lotus root.
Those with a Damp-Heat (Shi Re) constitution should strictly avoid alcohol, spicy dishes, deep-fried foods, beef, and warming tropical fruits like mango and longan, which aggravate internal heat.
Individuals with a Qi Deficiency (Qi Xu) constitution frequently suffer from shortness of breath, general fatigue, a weak voice, and spontaneous sweating with minimal exertion.
To boost energy and strengthen the spleen in a Qi Deficiency (Qi Xu) constitution, highly recommended foods include chicken, beef, red dates, sweet potato, Chinese yam, and ginseng.
People with a Qi Deficiency (Qi Xu) constitution should limit their intake of raw, cold-natured foods and heavy, greasy meals that require too much energy for the digestive system to process.
Individuals with a Blood Stasis (Xue Yu) constitution often have dull or dark complexions, dark lips, are prone to bruising easily, and may experience localized pain.
To promote blood circulation and remove stasis for a Blood Stasis (Xue Yu) constitution, beneficial foods include black beans, hawthorn fruit, brown sugar, wood ear mushroom, and rose tea.
Those with a Blood Stasis (Xue Yu) constitution should avoid highly processed foods, excessive salt, and very cold foods that constrict blood vessels and slow down metabolism.
Individuals with a Qi Stagnation (Qi Yu) constitution frequently feel emotionally stressed, depressed, have frequent sighs, and experience chest or abdominal bloating.
To regulate Qi flow and soothe the liver in a Qi Stagnation (Qi Yu) constitution, recommended foods include jasmine tea, citrus peel, onions, radishes, and hawthorn fruit.
People with a Qi Stagnation (Qi Yu) constitution should avoid heavy, difficult-to-digest foods and excessive caffeine, which can exacerbate physical and emotional tension.
Individuals with an Inherited Special (Te Bing) constitution have a highly sensitive system, often suffering from seasonal allergies, asthma, or chronic skin conditions like eczema.
To stabilize the immune system for an Inherited Special (Te Bing) constitution, a clean diet focusing on nutrient-dense foods like Lingzhi mushroom, honey, and fresh vegetables is recommended.
Those with an Inherited Special (Te Bing) constitution must strictly identify and avoid their specific allergens, as well as common triggers like seafood, alcohol, and artificial additives.
Individuals with a Neutral (Ping He) constitution represent a balanced state of health, characterized by good energy levels, normal sleep, a healthy appetite, and emotional stability.
For a Neutral (Ping He) constitution, the key is to maintain a balanced and diverse diet incorporating all food groups, eating in moderation, and following seasonal food availability.
People with a Neutral (Ping He) constitution do not need strict dietary restrictions but should avoid overeating, extreme diets, and excessive consumption of highly processed foods to maintain their balance.
